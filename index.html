Neural Radiance Field (NeRF) Project

This page documents my entire NeRF pipeline, from camera calibration to neural rendering, following the CS180 Project 4 structure.

Part 0.1 — Calibrating Your Camera

I captured checkerboard images and used OpenCV to compute intrinsic parameters and lens distortion coefficients.

Part 0.2 — Capturing a 3D Scan

I recorded a smooth rotation around the object to generate consistent multi-view observations.

Part 0.3 — Estimating Camera Pose

COLMAP recovered camera poses and a sparse reconstruction. The frustum visualization confirms the geometry is stable.

Part 0.4 — Undistorting & Preparing Dataset

I exported undistorted images and constructed the dataset format needed for NeRF training.

Part 1 — Fit a Neural Field to a 2D Image

I trained a small MLP to memorize an image by mapping coordinates to RGB values.

Training setup:
• Adam optimizer, MSE loss
• 2000 iterations
• Positional encoding L=10

Part 2.1 — Create Rays From Cameras

I converted every pixel into a world-space ray using camera intrinsics + extrinsics.

Part 2.2 — Sampling

I sampled points along each ray between near and far bounds.

Part 2.3 — Dataloading

I packed all ray bundles efficiently and indexed mini-batches during training.

Part 2.4 — NeRF Network

I implemented the NeRF architecture (coarse + fine models) with positional encoding.

Part 2.5 — Volume Rendering

I used alpha compositing to accumulate colors and densities into pixel intensities.

Part 2.6 — Training With My Dataset

I attempted to train a NeRF with Nerfstudio but encountered compatibility issues (COLMAP GPU flags on macOS). I completed all preprocessing steps, including dataset and transforms.json.
